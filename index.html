Qubit Count ≠ Quantum Performance

"100 high-quality qubits > 1000 noisy qubits"

Hardware Factors

  • Gate Fidelity: 99.9%+ needed for 2-qubit gates
  • Coherence Time: T1/T2 (100μs - 10ms)
  • Connectivity: Native topology matters
  • Error Rate: ~0.1-1% per gate
  • Noise: Gate, readout, crosstalk

System Factors

  • Circuit Depth: Limited by noise
  • Measurement: Readout fidelity
  • Calibration: Drift over time
  • Control: Real-time feedback
  • Scalability: Physical constraints

NISQ Era: Where We Are

Characteristics

  • 50-1000+ qubits
  • No error correction
  • Limited circuit depth
  • Noise accumulation
  • Short coherence times
  • Error mitigation needed

The NISQ Challenge

"Bridging the gap between NISQ and fault-tolerance"

  • Too many qubits for classical simulation
  • Too noisy for complex algorithms
  • Must find problems with quantum advantage
  • Need hybrid approaches

Quantum Annealing: An Alternative Hybrid Approach

What is Quantum Annealing?

  • Physical analog of optimization
  • Evolution from simple → problem Hamiltonian
  • D-Wave systems (2000+ qubits)
  • Not universal quantum computing

Advantages
